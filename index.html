The amazing time line of Stanley Martin Lieber

 
 
  • Born on December 28, 1922 in New York
    •  
  • Attended the DeWitt Clinton High School in The Bronx
    •  
  • He was hired as an office assistant at Timely Comics and soon became an interim editor.
    •  
  • Lee's entry into the Timely Comics in 1939 proved to be a big step in his career.
    •  
  • In 1941, Lee started getting opportunities to do actual comics with a backup feature.
    •  
  • When he was only 19 years old, Lee was made interim editor of the company.
    •  
  • In 1942, he joined the United States Army
    •  
  • After finishing his stint with the army, Lee joined back the company in 1950s.
    •  
  • In the late 1950, in competition with DC Comics, Lee's publisher, Goodman, asked him to create a new superhero team.
    •  
  • For this assignment, Lee, in association with his colleague Jack Kirby, created a team of superheroes called 'Fantastic Four' with superheroes like, 'Hulk', 'Iron Man', 'Thor', 'Spider-Man, 'X-Men', 'Doctor Strange', etc.
    •  
  • Throughout 1960s, he scripted, art-directed and edited most of Marvel's series
    •  
  • When Lee finished his 65 years with Marvel, it honored him in 2006 with the publication of a series of one-shot comics in which Lee was shown interacting with his co-creations like 'Spider-Man', 'Doctor Strange', etc.
    •  
  • Stan Lee died on on November 12, 2018, at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles, California, at the age of 95.
